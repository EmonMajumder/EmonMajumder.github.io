Katthak to Odishi

If dance is a mode of expression then classical dance forms are languages. I was taught to express my feeling through katthak however as I grew up, I understood that my language is not Kathak but in fact it is Odissi.

I started my journey with dancing at such a young age when I was not matured enough to understand the differences between the dance forms. I danced where my parents had admitted me, and the only classical dance form that I was taught in the dance school was katthak which is why neither did I have other options nor exposure to other forms.

As a young artist, I always enjoyed dancing with a freedom that flows through me, making me feel like I am flying leaving all the worldly troubles behind. I love the feeling of strength and freedom, soft yet swift, but in kathak I felt restrictions in every way.

In search of myself, I tried to learn other dance forms but I was never connected to them. I was always fascinated by the astounding body postures of the ancient sculptures and Odissi is a form which has been derived from them. I found it much easier to express myself through odissi. This dance form is everything I ever wanted to do with my body. I can feel myself more and be myself more. These new revelations about who I truly am, have allowed me to work on bridging these two dance form, but representing two different cultures.
